Hopefully this blog will become more detailed as my proficiency increases, but for now it remains pretty simple.

I'm going to use this blog to track my progress, outline short and long term goals, and note some tips and tricks to help me succeed.

WHY I AM NOW

When entering Skill Distillery, I didn't know much about coding. I ran into some of it at my previous two jobs, espcially as a QA tester.

I wanted to continue with coding because I saw more of a future in it than QA - I was a good tester but I could see that I wouldn't really

develop enough as a tester to satisfy my curiousity. Coding would use both sides of my brain, something I enjoyed doing as a tester.

Basically I'm going to reach my goal of landing a good programming gig by working hard. It's a bit of a cliche but I think my greatest

strength is my ability to put up with a bunch of crap better than most people.

WHERE I AM NOW

After going through most of the Java The Hard Way book (planning on finishing it) I think I have a pretty loose grasp of the basics

probably not enough to go rogue but enough to know how much I don't know. I'm ready to learn though!

I haven't coded much beyond what has been coded in the book, but I hope someday to code an app idea I have - The Regular. It's kind of

like an outsourced loyalty program that restaurants and bars can use to offer deals for repeat customers.

SOME THINGS TO BE CAREFUL FOR

In the past I have tried to push through when I got stuck and probably wasted more time than I should grinding my gears. I'm going to

try to be more proactive in asking for help when I get stuck. I am generally pretty good at not becoming too frustrated, but when I

do get frustrated, I tend to get frustrated about technology stuff - I hated setting up tech settings for different QA platforms to

only fail to get some APK to download. I'll try to keep my cool when some programming thing doesn't compile right.

When I do get frustrated, I have to step away from what I'm working on for a bit - this helps me not only control my frustration but

also helps me see a problem from a new angle. I sometimes have a tendancy to not always see the whole picture or to take for granted what

is not true.
